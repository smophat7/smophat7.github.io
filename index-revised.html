Spencer Moffat

Student software developer passionate about the intersection of neuroscience and computer science.

My Strangely Applicable Experience

Recently, I’ve come to the conclusion that having a uniquely varied background is not a hindrance, but an asset. I used to think that because I haven’t been coding since I was 14, I must be less than other developers. But I believe I make up for my relative lack of professional experience with my aptitude and passion for learning. Click around to learn more about my experiences.

On track to graduate in April 2022 from Brigham Young University with a bachelor’s in neuroscience and a minor in computer science. I have formal training in C++ and took the extra time I had this past summer to enroll in a linear algebra class based in Python as well as teach myself HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. This semester I’ll be learning how to use Vue, Node, Express, and MongoDB in a web development course.

In August 2020, I was hired as a web developer at BYU Campus Accommodations where I am adding SQL, ASP.NET, and AJAX to my toolbelt as I help the company transition to the model-driven software platform Mendix.

For a year, I worked in an addiction research laboratory on campus with the primary responsibility of performing intracranial injections on anesthetized mice. I got moved up and started training, learning more about instructing others in technical tasks, teamwork, and the life-and-death importance of attention to detail. The best part is that I can technically refer to myself as a former “neurosurgeon” if I really wanted to.

Working with a team of computer science and business students, I designed interactive mockups (using the aptly named Moqups) for a consulting company’s new PAAS focusing on intuitive and efficient functionality. I was the report manager too, so I oversaw the creation and delivery of our final reports to communicate our team's vision and recommendations. We masterfully managed the transition from remote work pre-COVID to remote work during COVID.

I co-authored a history of the Center for the Preservation of Ancient Religious Texts. I synthesized information from various reports, academic papers, interviews, and press articles to construct an informative, captivating article. I learned more about writing effectively, organizing vast amounts of information, and collaborating on a creative, yet technical project.
